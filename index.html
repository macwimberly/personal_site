In my prior life I was a client relationship guy at a too big to fail financial firm in San Francisco. I worked with clients in the ultra high net-worth segment. Many of these clients were very accomplished software engineers for compnaies like CISCO and GOOGLE. One of the best parts of my job was watching these folks build out new programs and start-ups. I had always had an interest in programming but despite taking a few computer science courses in college I never really pursued it. I ended up studying philosophy because I liked discussing the bigger picture questions. Programming seemed too granular to devote one's life to. Well, it turns out that there are no satisfying answers to those big questions. Perhaps I should have realized that when I noticed that we're still arguing about the same things as those back in 400BC, but as is often said, it's the journey not the destination.
